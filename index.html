One Semester at IIT Roorkee
A Reflection

When I first stepped into IIT Roorkee, I was both excited and overwhelmed. The campus buzzed with energy, students rushing to classes, cult clubs putting up posters for recruitments, and seniors telling us to do that, take part in that (that was really helpful though). I quickly realized that IIT was not just about lectures and exams; it was about immersing yourself in a culture where it is not only academics but really finding out what you are made for, finding out your interests and all that.One of the most defining experiences of my first semester was the spirit of competition. Suddenly, I found myself in an environment filled with people who, like me, had cleared the same challenging exam to be here. My batchmates werent just academically brilliant, they were excelling in a wide range of activities beyond the classroom, whether it be cult activity like dancing, singing, public speaking, fine arts or some techy field. I was not jealous of them, but I was always in a feeling of how much left behind I was. I could neither do dance, my singing was and is horrible, and about painting I did had interest in it when I was younger but never did painting after 7 grade I guess so, And you know these are already the perfect sowing grounds for FOMO (Fear of Missing Out) (a psychological phenomenon where someone feels anxious or uneasy about missing experiences, opportunities, or social events that others seem to be enjoying). At first, this constant comparison weighed heavily on me. Every fest, every club orientation, every late-night discussion in the hostel corridors seemed to remind me of the things I wasnt doing. But slowly, I began to realize that FOMO wasnt just about missing out on others experiences, it was also about not recognizing the value of my own journey. Instead of chasing every activity, I started focusing on what genuinely excited me. I found myself drawn to late-night coding sessions, exploring different fields and finding what i am actually made for. While others were rehearsing for stage performances, I usually used to be in library either alone or with my only best friend doing what really, I wanted to do.Over time, I reframed my perspective: rather than feeling left behind, I began to see myself as carving a different path. My peers inspired me, yes, but they no longer intimidated me. Their excellence pushed me to refine my oneself; to find joy in the things I could uniquely bring to the table.So yeah, that pretty much sums up my reflections on the first semester at IIT Roorkee
